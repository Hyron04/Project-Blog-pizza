Slice of Delight: Exploring the World Through Pizzas

The Origin of the Pizza

The origin of pizza traces back to ancient civilizations like the Greeks, Egyptians, and Romans, who ate flatbreads with various toppings.

However, the pizza as we know it today—with a dough base, tomato sauce, and cheese—originated in Naples, Italy, in the late 18th to early 19th century. Initially, pizza was a dish for poor people, sold in the streets and not considered a kitchen recipe.

It gained fame with the creation of the Margherita Pizza, which was named in honor of Queen Margherita of Savoy after her visit to Naples in 1889. The pizza was garnished with tomatoes, mozzarella cheese, and basil to represent the colors of the Italian flag. This event significantly boosted the dish's popularity, making it a symbol of Italian cuisine and later a global staple.

Different Pizzas

Pizzas, with their rich history and endless variations, offer a delicious exploration of global and regional tastes. Here’s a look at some distinct and popular types of pizzas from around the world:

  • Neapolitan Pizza (Naples, Italy): This is where pizza originated. Neapolitan pizza is known for its simple ingredients: a basic dough, raw tomatoes, fresh mozzarella cheese, fresh basil, and olive oil. No fancy toppings are allowed here; the charm is in the freshness and quality of the ingredients, baked in a wood-fired oven that gives it a unique, charred flavor that's hard to replicate.
  • New York Style Pizza (USA): Known for its large, foldable slices and crispy outer crust, New York-style pizza is made with a high-gluten bread flour and features a layer of tomato sauce and mozzarella cheese. It’s often sold by the slice to suit the fast-paced lifestyle of New Yorkers.
  • Chicago Deep Dish Pizza (USA): Unlike the thin crust of Neapolitan or New York-style pizzas, the Chicago deep dish is baked in a round, steel pan that gives it high edges. The toppings are layered in reverse, with slices of mozzarella lining the dough followed by meat, vegetables, and then canned tomato sauce on top. This pizza is quite hearty and thick, resembling more of a pie than a flatbread.
  • Sicilian Pizza (Sicily, Italy): Sicilian pizza, or "sfincione," features a thick crust and is traditionally topped with tomato sauce, onions, anchovies, and herbs. Over in the United States, the Sicilian style is often synonymous with a square-cut pizza that has a thick crust.
  • Detroit Style Pizza (USA): Characterized by its square shape and thick deep-dish crust which is crispy and chewy, Detroit-style pizza is unique because of its reverse topping style similar to Chicago deep dish. Traditionally, it’s topped with pepperoni, followed by brick cheese which goes all the way to the edges, creating a caramelized cheese border.
  • Roman Pizza (Rome, Italy): Roman pizzas come in two main types: pizza al taglio (by the slice) which is rectangular and served with a variety of toppings, and pizza Romana, a thinner, crispier but slightly softer than the Neapolitan version. Both are distinguished by their crust, which is airy and bubbly.
  • Calzone (Italy): Though not technically a pizza, it’s worth mentioning as it uses pizza dough. Calzones are crescent moon-shaped dough filled with the ingredients typically found on pizzas. The contents are enclosed entirely in the dough and it's either baked or fried, making it a delicious and portable meal.
  • Hawaiian Pizza (Canada): Despite its name, Hawaiian pizza was actually invented by a Canadian. It’s a standard pizza topped with cheese, ham, and pineapple, and it creates a controversial but popular sweet and savory flavor combination among pizza enthusiasts.
  • Indian Pizza: In India, local flavors make the pizza truly unique. Toppings might include tandoori chicken or paneer, and pickled ginger, with sauces that can range from traditional tomato to spicy masala. These pizzas are a fusion of Italian technique with Indian flavors.
  • Korean Pizza (South Korea): Korean pizza is often loaded with unique toppings that might include corn, sweet potato mousse, bulgogi (Korean BBQ beef), and even seafood such as shrimp or squid. It's a real departure from traditional Italian pizza, showcasing the playful and innovative nature of Korean cuisine.

These variations not only show the versatility of pizza but also how different cultures have embraced and personalized this beloved dish. Whether it’s the simplicity of a Margherita or the complexity of a Korean pizza, there’s a style to suit every palate.

Top three Pizza Restaurants in the World

The following Restaurants are celebrated for their exceptional pizza and have garnered significant acclaim in the culinary world.

  • Pepe in Grani (Caiazzo, Italy)
    • Chef: Franco Pepe
    • Specialty: Franco Pepe is revered for redefining what a pizza can be. At Pepe in Grani, the focus is on high-quality, locally sourced ingredients and an innovative approach to traditional pizza-making techniques. The pizzeria itself is located in a restored historic building, providing a charming ambiance.
    • Notable Pizza: One of their standout creations is the "Margherita Sbagliata," which includes buffalo mozzarella, yellow piennolo cherry tomatoes, basil, and local olive oil. The dough is noted for its perfect fermentation, which results in a light and flavorful base.
    • Recognition: While not a Michelin-starred restaurant, Pepe in Grani is often ranked among the best pizzerias in the world and has won numerous awards.
  • L’Antica Pizzeria da Michele (Naples, Italy)
    • History: Established in 1870, this pizzeria is one of the oldest and most revered in Naples. It has been run by the Condurro family for generations and remains a benchmark for traditional Neapolitan pizza.
    • Specialty: L'Antica Pizzeria da Michele sticks to simplicity and tradition, offering primarily two types of pizza: Margherita and Marinara.
    • Notable Pizza: Both Margherita and Marinara pizzas are celebrated for their authenticity and adherence to traditional Neapolitan pizza-making methods.
    • Recognition: The pizzeria gained international fame after being featured in the book and film "Eat, Pray, Love," where Julia Roberts's character declares it the best pizza in the world.
  • Pizzeria I Masanielli - Sasà Martucci (Caserta, Italy)
    • Chef: Sasà Martucci
    • Specialty: Known for innovative pizzas that incorporate unusual, high-quality ingredients from around Italy, Martucci's pizzeria has a modern approach while maintaining the core principles of traditional pizza making.
    • Notable Pizza: One standout creation is the pizza featuring black truffle and a variety of high-quality cheeses, showcasing the chef’s willingness to experiment with flavors while respecting the pizza’s integrity.
    • Recognition: The pizzeria has received numerous accolades for its high standards and innovative dishes, contributing to its reputation as one of the top pizza spots in Italy.

These establishments each offer a unique take on pizza, from staunchly traditional to innovatively modern, and are a testament to the versatility and global love for this dish. Each location not only serves excellent food but also tells a story through its pizzas, making them destinations worth visiting for any pizza enthusiast.
